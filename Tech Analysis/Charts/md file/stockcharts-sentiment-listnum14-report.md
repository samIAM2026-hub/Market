## Executive Summary

The sentiment backdrop is mixed. There is not a clean panic washout, but there is also not a fully euphoric extreme across every measure. Volatility is calm, total put/call has cooled back toward its lower band, and index-option protection is not elevated. Those are constructive while prices trend higher, but they leave less room for surprise if volatility turns up.

The main caution comes from positioning. NAAIM exposure is high, and the Rydex bear-plus-money-market to bull-assets ratio is low, which means investors and active managers appear fairly committed. At the same time, AAII remains net bearish, so the market still has some wall-of-worry support from retail skepticism.

The practical read: sentiment is supportive enough to keep the uptrend intact, but not washed out enough to cushion a sudden reversal. A VIX move back above 20 or renewed put/call spikes would be the most important early warning signals.

## Cautious Charts To Watch

**CAUTION: Positioning & Exposure** - NAAIM exposure is high near 84.7, and the Rydex defensive ratio is low near 0.06. That combination suggests investors are already leaning bullish, leaving less room for a positioning-driven upside surprise.

**CAUTION: VIX** - VIX is calm near 16.15 and sitting close to the 15 support zone. Calm volatility is friendly until it reverses; a move back above 20 would be an important risk-off warning.

**CAUTION: Total Put/Call Ratio** - $CPC has fallen back to the green lower reference zone near 0.79 after a recent fear spike. If it stays low while price weakens, that would signal complacency rather than healthy skepticism.

## Chart-by-Chart Analysis

### 1. **CAUTION: Positioning & Exposure - Put/Call, NAAIM, Rydex**

Local image: [Positioning & Exposure chart](stockcharts-sentiment-listnum14-01-000-positioning-exposure-put-call-naaim-rydex.png)

This combined chart tracks several sentiment and exposure gauges against the S&P 500. The top panel shows equity put/call behavior, the middle panel shows NAAIM exposure, and the bottom panel shows the Rydex bear-plus-money-market assets versus bullish assets.

The caution is in the lower two panels. NAAIM exposure is elevated near 84.7, while the Rydex defensive ratio is low near 0.06. That means active managers and fund flows appear meaningfully exposed to equities. This is not automatically bearish, but it does mean there may be less sidelined caution available to absorb a surprise selloff.

The equity put/call line has rebounded from an extremely complacent low, but it is not at panic levels. A deeper washout would usually require either a higher put/call spike or a larger pullback in exposure. Confirmation for bulls would be price strength while exposure remains steady; a warning would be falling prices with exposure still crowded.

### 2. **CAUTION: $VIX - Volatility Index**

Local image: [$VIX chart](stockcharts-sentiment-listnum14-02-001-vix-volatility-index.png)

VIX closed near 16.15 on July 2, above the green support line around 15 but below the orange 20 zone. The index has been making lower highs since the March volatility spike, so the volatility trend is still calmer than it was earlier in the year.

This is constructive for equities as long as VIX remains below 20. The caution is that VIX is near the lower end of its recent range, so the risk/reward of expecting further volatility compression is not as attractive. A close above 20 would mark a meaningful shift from calm tape to renewed stress.

The lower panel, VIX of VIX, is also subdued around 88.8. That supports the calm-volatility read, but it also means volatility expectations are quiet enough that a sharp expansion would likely matter.

### 3. **CAUTION: $CPC - Total Put/Call Ratio**

Local image: [$CPC chart](stockcharts-sentiment-listnum14-03-002-cpc-put-call-ratio-total-eod.png)

$CPC fell to about 0.79 after recently pushing close to 1.00. The 5-day average is still higher near 0.93, while the 50-day average sits around 0.84. This shows a short-term fear spike has already started to fade.

From a contrarian perspective, the current reading is not a major panic signal. It is closer to neutral-to-complacent, especially if it remains near the lower green reference line. Bulls would prefer price strength with $CPC staying orderly; bears would want to see $CPC remain low while the S&P 500 loses momentum.

### 4. $CPCE - Equity Only Put/Call Ratio

Local image: [$CPCE chart](stockcharts-sentiment-listnum14-04-003-cpce-put-call-ratio-equity-only-eod.png)

$CPCE dropped back toward 0.53 after a sharp rebound from the June complacency low. The 5-day average is near 0.54, so the short-term surge in equity put demand has eased quickly.

This is a mixed read. The June low showed too much complacency, but the recent rebound showed traders briefly reached for protection. The fast retreat back toward the mid-to-low zone means fear is not persistent. A move back above roughly 0.65-0.70 would show hedging demand returning; another slide toward 0.45-0.50 would warn of renewed complacency.

### 5. $CPCI - Index Only Put/Call Ratio

Local image: [$CPCI chart](stockcharts-sentiment-listnum14-05-004-cpci-put-call-ratio-index-only-eod.png)

$CPCI closed near 0.97, with the 5-day and 50-day averages near 1.06 and 1.04. The chart has lifted from the April-May lows but remains well below the major stress levels seen in late 2024 and April 2025.

This suggests institutional or index-level hedging demand is present but not extreme. A move back above 1.17 would be a stronger risk-off signal. Staying below the moving averages while equities hold trend is more consistent with normal hedging than panic.

### 6. AAII Bulls - Bears

Local image: [AAII Bulls-Bears chart](stockcharts-sentiment-listnum14-06-005-aaii-bulls-bears.png)

AAII bulls are near 31.4, while bears are near 42.3, leaving the bulls-minus-bears spread around -10.9. Retail sentiment remains net bearish even as the S&P 500 weekly chart is near highs and above its 13-week EMA and 40-week moving average.

This is the most constructive contrarian chart in the list. Skepticism has persisted through the price advance, which means the market is still climbing a wall of worry rather than being universally loved. The bullish confirmation would be the S&P 500 holding above the 13-week EMA near 7306 while the sentiment spread slowly improves.

The warning would be a weekly price reversal while AAII remains bearish. That would mean skepticism was not enough to provide support and could invite a test of the 40-week average near 6958.

## Cross-Chart Themes

Sentiment is not washed out. Put/call ratios are not at panic extremes, and VIX is closer to support than resistance. That means sentiment alone does not offer a strong contrarian buy signal.

Positioning is more crowded than the headline sentiment surveys suggest. NAAIM and Rydex imply investors are already fairly exposed, while AAII still shows retail skepticism. This split often means professional positioning is more vulnerable than survey sentiment.

The key confirmation is volatility. As long as VIX remains below 20, the sentiment backdrop can stay constructive. A VIX break above 20, especially with $CPC and $CPCE rising at the same time, would turn the sentiment tape more defensive.

## Bottom Line

The sentiment list leans cautiously constructive. There is enough skepticism in AAII to support the uptrend, but positioning and low volatility argue against complacency. The highest-priority warning levels are VIX above 20, $CPC pushing back toward 1.05, and $CPCE rising above 0.65-0.70 while equities weaken.
