## Executive Summary

The US index picture is broadly constructive, but leadership has rotated. The strongest-looking charts are Dow Industrials, NYSE Composite, Value Line Arithmetic, small caps, micro caps, and Russell 2000. These charts are above rising 20-day, 50-day, and 200-day moving averages, with RSI generally holding in bullish territory.

The main caution is the Nasdaq complex. The Nasdaq Composite and Nasdaq 100 have pulled back from June highs, are sitting around or below short moving averages, and have RSI readings below 50. That is not full trend damage because both remain far above rising 200-day averages, but it does show near-term loss of momentum in the prior growth leadership group.

The broad market message is healthier than a narrow-cap-weighted read would suggest. Smaller-cap and equal-weight-style proxies are participating, which supports breadth. Confirmation would be Nasdaq reclaiming its 20-day/50-day averages while the broader indexes hold above support. The warning would be small caps and Value Line rolling over after their recent strength.

## Cautious Charts To Watch

- **$COMPQ - Nasdaq Composite:** Price is below the 20-day and 50-day moving averages, and RSI is just under 50.
- **$NDX - Nasdaq 100 Index:** Similar to the Composite, with RSI below 50 and price testing the 20-day/50-day area after a sharp June pullback.
- **$OEX - S&P 100 Index:** Reclaiming short averages, but still below the prior high zone and less convincing than Dow/NYSE/small-cap charts.
- **IWB / IWV:** Broad large-cap ETFs are constructive but still rebuilding from June weakness; they need follow-through above recent swing highs.

## Cross-Chart Themes

Trend: Most charts are above their 20-day, 50-day, and 200-day moving averages. The dominant intermediate trend is still positive.

Leadership: Dow Industrials, NYSE Composite, Value Line, micro caps, small caps, and Russell 2000 are showing the healthiest technical profiles. This suggests broader participation below the mega-cap layer.

Momentum: Momentum is strongest in Dow Industrials, Value Line, NYSE Composite, utilities, and small/micro caps. Nasdaq Composite and Nasdaq 100 are the notable momentum laggards, with RSI below 50.

Breadth/size rotation: Smaller and broader equal-weight-style proxies are outperforming the large-cap growth leadership group in the near term. That is a constructive rotation if it persists.

Risk: The main risk is a failed repair in Nasdaq and cap-weighted large caps. If Nasdaq loses the 50-day area while small caps also roll over, the broad-market message would deteriorate quickly.

## Bottom Line

The US index ChartList is broadly bullish, but the leadership mix has changed. The healthiest read comes from the Dow, NYSE Composite, Value Line Arithmetic Index, small caps, micro caps, and Russell 2000. These charts support the idea that the rally is broadening.

The main caution remains Nasdaq and large-cap growth. $COMPQ and $NDX need to reclaim their short moving averages and turn RSI back above 50. If that happens while small caps hold their gains, the market would have a much more balanced bullish profile. If Nasdaq fails and the broader indexes start losing their 20-day/50-day averages, the current rotation would become a warning rather than a strength.
