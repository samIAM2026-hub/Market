## Executive Summary

The breadth backdrop is constructive, but not uniformly powerful. NYSE breadth is healthier than Nasdaq breadth: the NYSE advance-decline chart shows more advancing issues than declining issues, a rising index above its 50-day and 200-day averages, and a positive McClellan structure. Nasdaq breadth is improving but more mixed, with more declining than advancing issues on the latest reading and the Nasdaq Composite sitting near its 50-day average.

New highs are expanding again across major index groups, while new lows remain contained. The net new-high minus new-low charts are positive across NYSE, Nasdaq, S&P 500, mid caps, small caps, and Nasdaq 100. This supports the idea that the rally is broadening rather than being driven only by a narrow leadership group.

The caution is that neither Zweig Breadth Thrust chart shows a confirmed thrust signal. Breadth is supportive, but the charts do not yet show the kind of synchronized upside surge that would mark a fresh, high-conviction breadth thrust.

## Cautious Charts To Watch

- **001 - NASDAQ Advance-Decline:** Daily Nasdaq breadth is negative, with declining issues above advancing issues and the Nasdaq Composite sitting near its 50-day average.
- **003 - New Lows (Major Indexes):** New lows are broadly contained, but Nasdaq still has the most noticeable new-low count and should be watched for expansion.
- **006 - NASDAQ McClellan Indicators:** The oscillator is positive, but the Nasdaq McClellan Summation Index has not fully reclaimed its 50-day average.
- **007 - Zweig Breadth Thrust - NYSE:** Supportive breadth, but no confirmed thrust signal yet.
- **008 - Zweig Breadth Thrust - NASDAQ:** Mildly positive breadth, but no confirmed thrust signal and Nasdaq price remains close to its 50-day average.

The NYSE McClellan Oscillator closed near **+21.95**, above zero and recovering from its June weakness. The NYSE Composite is trending above its rising 50-day average, and the McClellan Summation Index is near **316.17**, just above its 50-day average near **315.52**.

The signal is constructive but not overheated. Breadth momentum is positive, and the summation index has repaired enough to sit slightly above its smoothing average. Confirmation would be continued positive oscillator readings and a rising summation index. Weakening would be a rollover below zero in the oscillator and a summation index break back below its 50-day average.

## Cross-Chart Themes

Trend: The NYSE Composite and Nasdaq Composite remain above their 200-day moving averages, so the larger trend is still positive.

Participation: NYSE participation is stronger than Nasdaq participation. NYSE advancing issues outnumber declining issues, while Nasdaq declining issues slightly outnumber advancing issues on the latest reading.

Highs and lows: New highs are expanding across most major groups, and new lows are contained. The net new-high minus new-low panels are positive across all six visible groups.

Momentum: NYSE McClellan readings are positive and repaired. Nasdaq McClellan readings are improving but not fully confirmed because the Summation Index remains just below its 50-day average.

Thrust: Neither the NYSE nor Nasdaq Zweig Breadth Thrust chart shows a confirmed digital thrust signal. The rally has breadth support, but not a full breadth-thrust confirmation.

## Bottom Line

Breadth supports the equity uptrend, especially on the NYSE side. The healthiest evidence is the combination of positive new-high minus new-low readings, contained new lows, and a repaired NYSE McClellan profile.

The main caution is Nasdaq. Nasdaq has strong new-high counts, but its daily advance-decline reading is negative, its Composite is near the 50-day average, and its McClellan Summation Index has not fully reclaimed its 50-day average. The next bullish confirmation would be Nasdaq breadth joining NYSE breadth with advancing issues above 0.50, a Composite close back above the 50-day, and a rising Summation Index. The key warning would be expanding Nasdaq new lows and McClellan deterioration while price fails at the 50-day.
