## Executive Summary

The cap-weight sector view shows a market still led by offensive and cyclical leadership, but with a few obvious weak links. Technology remains the dominant long-term relative leader, Industrials are in a clean uptrend, Financials have improved sharply, and Health Care just produced one of the strongest recent breakouts in the group.

The main caution is that leadership is not evenly distributed. Communication Services is the standout laggard, trading below its 50-day and 200-day moving averages with a deeply negative relative-strength line. Energy has lost momentum after a strong first-quarter advance, and Consumer Discretionary remains choppy with poor relative strength despite holding near its 200-day average.

The S&P 500 proxy, SPY, is still above its 50-day and 200-day moving averages, so the broad cap-weight trend remains constructive. The best confirmation would be SPY holding above the 735-741 support zone while more sectors participate. The warning would be continued narrow leadership while XLC, XLE, and XLY fail to repair.

## Cautious Charts To Watch

**CAUTION: XLC - Communication Services** - This is the weakest chart in the set. Price is below the 50-day and 200-day averages, relative strength versus SPY is around -14.9%, and the late-June bounce has not yet repaired the breakdown.

**CAUTION: XLE - Energy** - Energy is still above its 200-day average, but it is below the 20-day and 50-day averages and relative strength has rolled down from a major first-quarter peak. The current bounce needs to reclaim roughly 54.7-56.7 to improve.

**CAUTION: XLY - Consumer Discretionary** - XLY is stuck in a wide range and its relative-strength line is still deeply negative, near -11.6% versus SPY. It needs to hold the 200-day area near 117 and break back above 119-122 to look healthy.

**WATCH: XLP - Consumer Staples** - Price is above all three moving averages, but relative strength remains weak near -12.5%. It is not a broken price chart, but it is still not confirming broad leadership.

## Cross-Chart Themes

Leadership is selective. Technology remains the long-term relative leader, Industrials are steady, Financials are improving, and Health Care has just broken out. That is enough to support the cap-weight S&P 500, especially because several large sectors are participating.

The laggards are concentrated in Communication Services, Energy, and Consumer Discretionary. XLC is outright weak, XLE has lost intermediate momentum, and XLY remains range-bound with poor relative strength. If these three repair, sector breadth would improve quickly.

Defensive sectors are not uniformly bearish. Staples, Utilities, and Real Estate are holding above key moving averages, but their relative-strength lines remain negative. That suggests defensive groups are stabilizing, not yet leading.

## Bottom Line

The sector picture is constructive but uneven. SPY remains above key moving averages, and leadership from XLK, XLI, XLF, and XLV is supportive. The main warning signs are XLC below its 50-day and 200-day averages, XLE below its short/intermediate averages, and XLY sitting on its 200-day while still lagging badly versus SPY.
