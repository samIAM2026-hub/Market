## Executive summary

### Market regime: constructive primary trend, tactical caution

The June 26 charts do not show a broad bearish breakdown, but they also do not restore a clean bullish tape. The market remains in a **constructive primary trend with tactical stress**.

The S&P 500 closed at **7,354.02**, slightly below its 50-day average near **7,363.43** and below its 21-day EMA near **7,418.26**. That keeps the daily chart under pressure. On the weekly chart, SPY closed at **728.99**, below its 5-week EMA near **736.73**, but still above the 13-, 21-, and 34-week EMAs. The message is therefore split by timeframe: the intermediate and long-term uptrend is still alive, while the short-term trend remains vulnerable.

The bullish offset is breadth. The NYSE, S&P 500, mid-cap, and small-cap advance-decline lines remain above their 50-day averages. Around **65.2%** of S&P 500 stocks are above their 200-day averages, and **64.6%** are above their 50-day averages. RSP/SPY remains above both its 50- and 200-day averages, and IWM/SPY improved to **0.411**, also above both key averages. That is not the internal profile of a market that has already broadly broken down.

The main tactical warnings are money flow, Nasdaq breadth, and risk-appetite cooling. SPY Chaikin Money Flow remains negative near **-0.124**. The Nasdaq 100 Bullish Percent Index improved to **38**, but that is still below the important 50 level. High beta versus low volatility fell sharply on the day, and stocks versus bonds remain below their 50-day average. VIX traded above 20 intraday but closed at **18.41**, so volatility flashed a warning but did not confirm a panic regime.

Credit and bond volatility remain stabilizers. High-yield spreads were still calm near **2.78**, and MOVE closed near **66.79**. Major equity breaks are more dangerous when price, breadth, volatility, credit, and rates volatility deteriorate together. As of this June 26 refresh, that full stress confirmation is not present.

Leadership is selective. Semiconductors remain in a powerful relative uptrend, but SMH/SPY fell sharply to **0.839** after being very extended. Small caps and equal weight continue to argue for broadening. Broad growth versus value bounced but remains below its 50-day average, while pure growth/value remains firmer. The cleaner description is **selective broadening with semiconductor leadership, but not a fully synchronized risk-on tape**.

### Key conclusion

The most important market line is still the S&P 500’s 50-day area. A quick reclaim of the 50-day average near **7,363** and the 21-day EMA near **7,418** would repair the short-term trend. A decisive failure below the June 26 low near **7,294**, especially with VIX holding above **20**, negative CMF, and weakening breadth, would shift the dashboard toward deeper correction risk.

## Market regime / thesis

The market is still better described as a **support test inside an uptrend** than as a confirmed bearish reversal. Long-term charts, credit conditions, breadth, equal weight, and small caps remain supportive. The caution is that short-term price is below the 21-day EMA, money flow is negative, Nasdaq breadth is weak, and several risk-on ratios have cooled.

The thesis is therefore disciplined rather than aggressively bullish: stay aware that the primary trend is intact, but demand confirmation from price, volatility, and money flow before treating the pullback as fully resolved.

# Cross-chart conclusions

## 1. Price is the problem; breadth is the offset

The S&P 500 is below the 21-day EMA and slightly below the 50-day average, so price needs repair. But breadth has not broken: advance-decline lines, percentage-above-moving-average readings, equal weight, and small caps remain constructive.

That combination usually deserves patience but not complacency.

## 2. Leadership is broadening, but uneven

Small caps and equal weight are the best broadening evidence. Semiconductors remain the strongest leadership engine. Value/cyclical groups have improved. But broad growth/value, discretionary/staples, Nasdaq breadth, and stocks-versus-bonds are not fully confirming.

This is selective leadership, not synchronized risk-on.

## 3. Credit and rates volatility are not confirming panic

High-yield spreads remain calm and MOVE is low. That keeps the downside risk from becoming a full systemic-stress call. If credit spreads widen while VIX holds above 20, the dashboard would become meaningfully more defensive.

## 4. Commodities and crypto remain weak

Gold bounced but remains below key averages. Crude remains below the 200-day average. Bitcoin remains below both the 50- and 200-day averages. Cross-asset risk appetite is therefore less convincing than equity breadth alone suggests.
