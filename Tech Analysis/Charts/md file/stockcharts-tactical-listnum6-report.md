## Executive Summary

The list shows a mixed but not broken tactical tape. Most major equity proxies staged a strong rebound from the late-June lows into July 1, then pulled back sharply on July 2. The key question is whether the 5-day moving average, shown as the orange 65-period average on these 30-minute charts, becomes support again or turns into resistance.

The Dow Industrials are the clear relative leader, still above a rising 5-day average and pressing near the top of the one-month range. The S&P 500, S&P 100, Total Market, and Russell 1000 are more neutral-to-constructive after bouncing back toward or above the average late in the session.

The caution is concentrated in Nasdaq 100, mid caps, small caps, and Russell 2000. Those charts broke below the 5-day average after strong runs, and their rebounds remain tentative unless they reclaim the average and prior short-term breakdown zones.

## Cautious Charts To Watch

**CAUTION: Nasdaq 100 / QQQ** - The sharp July 2 drop left QQQ below its 5-day average, with a lower-high sequence from the July 1 peak. It needs to reclaim roughly the 721-722 moving-average area first, then the 724-728 breakdown zone, to neutralize the short-term damage.

**CAUTION: S&P 400 Mid Cap / $MID** - Mid caps fell decisively below the 5-day average and only bounced modestly from the 3766 area. A recovery through roughly 3820-3825 would improve the setup; failure there keeps the short-term trend defensive.

**CAUTION: S&P 600 Small Cap / $SML** - Small caps broke below the 5-day average after peaking near 1817 and are still below the average near 1792. A close back above that area would matter; below 1767-1762 would suggest the pullback is expanding.

**CAUTION: Russell 2000 / IWM** - IWM is below its 5-day average near 299 after a retreat from the 302-303 zone. The bounce from 294.98 helps, but it has not yet repaired the short-term breakdown.

## Chart-by-Chart Analysis

### 1. Total Market / VTI

Local image: [VTI chart](../Charts/stockcharts-tactical-listnum6-01-01-total-market.png)

VTI recovered strongly from the late-June low near 359.95 into a July 1 high near 372.24, then gave back part of that move on July 2. The late-session bounce lifted price back above the orange 5-day average around 367.47, which keeps the chart from turning outright bearish.

The setup is neutral-to-constructive as long as VTI holds above the 366.18-367.47 support band. A push back above 369.5-370.5 would show the pullback is being absorbed; a failure back below 366 would warn that the July 1 high may have started a deeper tactical retracement.

### 2. S&P 100 / $OEX

Local image: [$OEX chart](../Charts/stockcharts-tactical-listnum6-02-02-s-p-100.png)

$OEX is holding up better than the broader small/mid-cap groups. After a spike toward 3696.09, it fell back toward the 3645 area, then rebounded to finish above the 5-day average near 3643.27.

That late recovery keeps the large-cap leadership read intact. Bulls want to see $OEX hold above 3643-3645 and retest 3671-3696. A decisive break back below the average would shift this from leadership to a failed bounce.

### 3. S&P 500 / $SPX

Local image: [$SPX chart](../Charts/stockcharts-tactical-listnum6-03-03-s-p-500.png)

$SPX is similar to $OEX but a little less clean. It rallied hard from the June 26 low near 7294 into a July 2 spike near 7540, then reversed toward the 7427 area before recovering late.

The index finished above the 5-day average near 7447, so the tactical uptrend is bruised rather than broken. Holding 7427-7448 is important. A move back above 7508-7541 would confirm renewed upside momentum; losing 7427 would put the late-June breakout at risk.

### 4. **CAUTION: S&P 400 Mid Cap / $MID**

Local image: [$MID chart](../Charts/stockcharts-tactical-listnum6-04-04-s-p-400.png)

$MID is one of the weaker tactical charts. It peaked near 3861 on July 1, then fell below the 5-day average near 3820 and finished around 3803 after only a modest rebound.

The recent candles show a lower-high/lower-low pattern since the peak. Reclaiming 3820-3825 would be the first repair signal, while a break below 3766 would confirm that sellers remain in control on this short-term timeframe.

### 5. **CAUTION: S&P 600 Small Cap / $SML**

Local image: [$SML chart](../Charts/stockcharts-tactical-listnum6-05-05-s-p-600.png)

$SML had a strong late-June advance, but the July 2 action pushed it below the 5-day average near 1792. The chart is still above the late-June base, but the clean short-term uptrend has been interrupted.

The key upside repair level is 1792-1800, where the moving average and breakdown area sit. If $SML cannot reclaim that zone, the next downside watch area is roughly 1767-1762.

### 6. **CAUTION: Nasdaq 100 / QQQ**

Local image: [QQQ chart](../Charts/stockcharts-tactical-listnum6-06-06-nasdaq-100.png)

QQQ is the weakest large-cap chart in this list. After a rebound into the 737-738 zone, it formed a lower high and then dropped sharply below the 5-day average near 721.66, finishing around 712.60.

This is a defensive short-term signal. A bounce above 721-722 is needed just to reclaim the moving average, and a move through 724-728 would help repair the failed breakout attempt. Below 707.56, the chart would be vulnerable to a retest of the 704-703 area.

### 7. Russell 1000 / IWB

Local image: [IWB chart](../Charts/stockcharts-tactical-listnum6-07-07-russell-1000.png)

IWB sits between the stronger large-cap indexes and weaker smaller-cap charts. It briefly broke below the 5-day average near 406.81, then recovered to close around 408.75.

That reclaim is constructive, but not decisive. Holding 406-407 keeps the recovery case alive. A push back above 410.25-412.17 would show buyers regaining control; losing 405.84 would put the late-June rally under pressure.

### 8. **CAUTION: Russell 2000 / IWM**

Local image: [IWM chart](../Charts/stockcharts-tactical-listnum6-08-08-russell-2000.png)

IWM rolled over from the 302-303 area and broke below the 5-day average near 298.79. The rebound from 294.98 is constructive, but the chart has not yet reclaimed the moving average.

The first bullish repair level is 298.8-300.0. Until that area is recovered, the Russell 2000 remains tactically cautious. A break below 294.98 would point to a deeper test of the late-June support cluster near 292.4-294.6.

### 9. Dow Industrials / $INDU

Local image: [$INDU chart](../Charts/stockcharts-tactical-listnum6-09-09-dow-industrials.png)

$INDU is the strongest chart in the set. It held above a rising 5-day average near 52,302 and pushed to a fresh short-term high near 52,900 by the end of the chart.

This is the cleanest leadership signal. As long as the Dow holds above 52,300-52,400, the tactical trend remains constructive. A loss of that band would not be fatal, but it would bring the chart back in line with the more mixed large-cap readings elsewhere.

## Cross-Chart Themes

Large-cap breadth is still acceptable, but leadership is narrow. The Dow, S&P 100, S&P 500, Total Market, and Russell 1000 have either held or reclaimed the 5-day average, while Nasdaq 100 and smaller-cap indexes are below it.

The July 2 pullback matters because it arrived after several indexes had just pressed into short-term highs. A quick reclaim of the moving averages would frame the drop as normal digestion. Failure to reclaim them would turn the pattern into a broader tactical warning.

Small and mid caps are the main risk tell. If $MID, $SML, and IWM continue to lag while the Dow holds up, the tape is rotating defensively rather than confirming broad risk appetite.

## Bottom Line

The market is not uniformly weak, but the tactical picture is split. Dow leadership and late rebounds in VTI, $OEX, $SPX, and IWB keep the bullish case alive. The caution flags are QQQ, $MID, $SML, and IWM, all of which need to reclaim their 5-day averages to restore a healthier short-term setup.
