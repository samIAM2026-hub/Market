Executive Summary Show

Market Regime — Constructive Primary Trend, Tactical Caution

The June 26 charts do not show a broad bearish breakdown, but they also do not restore a clean bullish tape. The market remains in a constructive primary trend with tactical stress — a support test inside an uptrend rather than a confirmed reversal.

The S&P 500 closed at 7,354.02, slightly below its 50-day average near 7,363.43 and below its 21-day EMA near 7,418.26, keeping the daily chart under pressure. On the weekly chart, SPY closed at 728.99, below its 5-week EMA near 736.73, but still above the 13-, 21-, and 34-week EMAs. The message is split by timeframe: the intermediate and long-term uptrend is alive, while the short-term trend remains vulnerable.

The bullish offset is breadth. The NYSE, S&P 500, mid-cap, and small-cap advance-decline lines remain above their 50-day averages. Around 65.2% of S&P 500 stocks are above their 200-day averages, and 64.6% are above their 50-day averages. RSP/SPY remains above both its 50- and 200-day averages, and IWM/SPY improved to 0.411, also above both key averages. That is not the internal profile of a market that has already broadly broken down.

The main tactical warnings are money flow, Nasdaq breadth, and risk-appetite cooling. SPY Chaikin Money Flow remains negative near -0.124. The Nasdaq 100 Bullish Percent Index improved to 38, but is still below the important 50 level. High beta versus low volatility fell sharply on the day, and stocks versus bonds remain below their 50-day average. VIX traded above 20 intraday but closed at 18.41, so volatility flashed a warning without confirming a panic regime.

Credit and bond volatility remain stabilizers. High-yield spreads were still calm near 2.78, and MOVE closed near 66.79. Major equity breaks are more dangerous when price, breadth, volatility, credit, and rates volatility deteriorate together. As of this June 26 refresh, that full stress confirmation is not present.

Leadership Read

Leadership is selective. Semiconductors remain in a powerful relative uptrend, but SMH/SPY fell sharply to 0.839 after being very extended. Small caps and equal weight continue to argue for broadening, and value cyclicals (industrials, financials, materials) have improved from earlier lows.

The offset is that broad growth versus value bounced but remains below its 50-day average, while pure growth/value stays firmer. The cleaner description is selective broadening with semiconductor leadership, but not a fully synchronized risk-on tape.

Cross-Asset Message

Cross-asset charts reinforce the selective tone. The U.S. dollar is firm, while gold bounced but remains technically damaged, crude stays below its 200-day average, and Bitcoin remains below both its 50- and 200-day averages. Stocks vs. bonds (SPY/TLT) has slipped below its 50-day average, weakening the tactical allocation signal. Cross-asset risk appetite is less convincing than equity breadth alone suggests.

Key Conclusion & Thesis

The most important market line is still the S&P 500's 50-day area. A quick reclaim of the 50-day average near 7,363 and the 21-day EMA near 7,418 would repair the short-term trend. A decisive failure below the June 26 low near 7,294, especially with VIX holding above 20, negative CMF, and weakening breadth, would shift the dashboard toward deeper correction risk.

The thesis is therefore disciplined rather than aggressively bullish: stay aware that the primary trend is intact, but demand confirmation from price, volatility, and money flow before treating the pullback as fully resolved.

Bottom Line

The June 26 refresh leaves the market in a constructive but tactically fragile position. The long-term trend, credit backdrop, rates volatility, equal weight, small caps, and broad participation measures remain supportive. The caution comes from the S&P 500's failure to reclaim its short-term averages, negative money flow, weak Nasdaq breadth, risk-appetite cooling, and crowded professional exposure.

The dashboard does not justify an outright bearish regime call yet. It does justify a tighter, more conditional bullish view: the market needs to reclaim the 50-day and 21-day areas quickly, or the June 26 support test can evolve into a deeper correction.

Monitoring checklist: S&P 500 — 50-day near 7,363, 21-day EMA near 7,418, June 26 low near 7,294; VIX behavior around 20; SPY CMF crossing back above or staying below zero; S&P 500 percent above 50-/200-day averages; Nasdaq 100 BPI vs. 50; SMH/SPY holding its 50-day relative average; IWM/SPY and RSP/SPY above their 50-/200-day averages; high-yield spreads calm vs. widening; SPY/TLT vs. its 50-day average.
