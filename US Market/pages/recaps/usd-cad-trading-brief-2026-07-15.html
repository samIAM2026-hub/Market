5. Bank of Canada Analysis

Verified facts On July 15 the Bank held at 2.25% for a sixth straight meeting. Growth is expected to strengthen in the second half as inflation eases. Markets priced about 15 bps of tightening by December, down from 17 bps before the decision. BoC decision/MPR; Reuters.

Guidance: Governor Tiff Macklem said a persistent oil shock could require higher rates, but that is not the base case. The Bank sees choppy output, signs growth resumed in Q2 and broadening momentum later. The next move depends on inflation persistence. Press conference.

Six-to-twelve months: the curve implies less than one full hike in 2026. The practical range is hold to one hike, with cuts returning only if trade or household stress materially weakens demand.

At about 1.4031, the rate is above the 2025 year-end reference but near the lower edge of the recent one-week range. Conditions do not clearly favor converting all at once or waiting entirely; staging can reduce event and regret risk.
