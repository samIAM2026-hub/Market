1. Executive Summary

  • Dominant narrative: June inflation cooled more than expected, reversing much of Monday's oil-driven rise in yields and allowing semiconductor and long-duration growth shares to rebound.
  • S&P 500 +0.38%; Nasdaq +0.90%; the Dow added only 0.02% because IBM's 25.2% collapse offset strength elsewhere.
  • Headline CPI fell 0.4% m/m and slowed to 3.5% y/y; core CPI was unchanged and eased to 2.6% y/y, both softer than expected. BLS
  • Treasury yields fell 8 bp at two years to 4.18% and 4 bp at ten years to 4.58%; July-hike odds fell below 17% from roughly 42% Monday. Treasury
  • AI hardware led: Micron +4.9%, Nvidia +4.1%, semiconductor proxy about +2.6%.
  • Earnings differentiated: five large banks beat, Goldman +9%, Citi −5.3%; IBM missed preliminary revenue and EPS expectations.
  • Takeaway: CPI weakened the immediate hike case, but June energy relief predates July's renewed Hormuz shock. PPI and Warsh testimony are next.
Evidence: index closes, BLS data, official yields and IBM's disclosure are confirmed facts. Attributed causal explanations are sourced commentary; labeled interpretations are this report's analysis.

3. Market Drivers

  1. CPI broke the hawkish loop: headline and core undershot, lowering yields, DXY and July-hike odds.
  2. AI hardware rebounded: Nvidia and Micron recovered; interpretation—rates relief reopened duration exposure without settling AI-return questions.
  3. Bank earnings confirmed resilience: strong trading, spending and contained delinquencies; reactions varied by business mix.
  4. IBM counterweighted the index: $17.2B preliminary revenue and $2.93 adjusted EPS missed; shares fell 25.2%.
  5. Oil limited relief: Brent +1.7% as Hormuz risk persisted, despite paring its high after the proposed cargo charge was withdrawn.

5. Rates, Bonds, and the U.S. Dollar

Two-year yields fell 4.26%→4.18%; ten-year 4.62%→4.58%. The 2s10s curve steepened to about +40 bp because the front end rallied faster.

The ten-year's smaller fall reflected softer current inflation versus oil/fiscal term premium; it remains above 3.97% before the Iran war. Tuesday was relief, not full reversal.

DXY fell about 0.4% toward 100.90. Lower yields/dollar helped growth and gold. No material auction or broad credit dislocation drove the session.

7. Major Companies and Earnings

IBM

IBM expects $17.2B revenue (+1%) versus ~$17.86B expected and $2.93 adjusted EPS versus $3.01. Software +5%, consulting flat, infrastructure −7%; YTD free cash flow $4.8B. Management cited spending shifts to servers/storage/memory and slipped deals. Shares fell 25.2%. IBM letter

Banks

  • JPMorgan: $16.9B profit, $6.14 EPS vs $5.59, $58B managed revenue; markets +35%, equities +86%, investment banking +30%.
  • Goldman: $6.6B profit, $20.98 EPS, $20.3B revenue; banking/markets $15.52B, +53%; shares +9%.
  • Wells: income +22% to $6.4B, $22.6B revenue beat; spending higher/delinquencies lower; shares fell.
  • BofA: profit +27% to $9.1B; spending, deposits and assets rose.
  • Citi: revenue/profit beat, markets +45%, shares −5.3%.

Nvidia +4.1% and Micron +4.9%; macro rates relief and infrastructure allocation both helped.

8. Market Breadth and Leadership

Russell +0.39% matched S&P +0.38%, so participation was not exclusively megacap. Nasdaq +0.90%, tech leadership and defensive losses show strongest buying concentrated in growth/chips.

A synchronized equal-weight close and final exchange breadth were unavailable. Verifiable evidence supports selective risk-on / growth rebound, not a fully broad rally.

9. Cross-Asset Confirmation

Treasuries, DXY, gold and VIX confirmed CPI relief: yields/dollar fell, gold rebounded, VIX −3.85% to 16.50.

Oil contradicted clean disinflation: Brent +1.7% at $84.73. June energy relief reverses if July crude/gasoline remain elevated. Natural gas did not signal generalized shortage; no verified industrial-metal move or credit stress confirmed a broader shock. Crypto is excluded as immaterial to cash-session sentiment.

10. Global and Geopolitical Context

Nikkei +0.7%, SoftBank +3.3%; Shanghai +1.4% after China exports +27% y/y, partly chip-driven. This improved the U.S. chip backdrop after Monday's Korean rout. Europe was modestly positive, so U.S. gains were domestic-CPI/earnings led.

Hormuz risk lifts oil, freight, insurance, inflation expectations and yields. Tuesday displayed current CPI relief and forward oil risk together. Withdrawal of the proposed cargo charge trimmed crude's high but did not resolve access/security. A softer dollar marginally eased emerging-market conditions; inference—secondary support.

Key Event Previews

PPI/Warsh: trade-services and healthcare inputs feed PCE. Hot PPI plus hawkish testimony reverses Tuesday's front-end rally; soft PPI plus data dependence reinforces hold pricing.

Hot/hawkish: two-year yield and dollar rebound; growth gives back leadership.
In line: CPI stays dominant; earnings drive dispersion.
Cool/balanced: hike pricing recedes; growth/housing benefit.

Retail: the GDP control group matters more than gasoline. Strong real sales support growth but lift yields; weakness challenges the bank consumer narrative.

Housing/production/sentiment: starts test rate-sensitive construction; production separates manufacturing from energy/utilities; Michigan expectations show whether oil changes household psychology.

  • Thursday: TSMC, PepsiCo, Netflix, Alcoa; watch AI capacity/margins, ads, power costs and demand.

13. Bottom Line

Regime: selective risk-on with a disinflation impulse, constrained by oil and earnings dispersion. Core CPI and shelter give the move a firmer foundation, but durability depends on PPI confirmation and Brent not lifting expectations. Unresolved: AI breadth, whether IBM is isolated, and whether the Fed treats oil as temporary.
