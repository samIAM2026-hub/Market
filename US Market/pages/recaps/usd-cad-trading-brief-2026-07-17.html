  • Spot: USD/CAD was about 1.4020 at the cutoff after trading 1.4005–1.4041. The vendor day convention showed −0.13%, or −18.1 pips from a 1.4038 open. Myfxbook.

  • Short-term trend: bearish USD/CAD below 1.4050; the pair has made lower daily highs since July 13.
  • Medium-term trend: mixed: spot is below the 20-day average but remains above 50-, 100- and 200-day averages on one daily-data source.

  • Next 24–72 hours: Canada June CPI on Monday, July 20 at 5:30 a.m. PDT / 8:30 a.m. ET is the main event risk.
  • Bias: neutral with downward risk while below 1.4050; a daily close under 1.3990 would convert the weekly decline into a clearer bearish break.

DXY ended almost flat near 100.73. Housing starts jumped to 1.427m and sentiment to 54.4, but industrial output rose only 0.1%, capacity use was 76.1%, and one-year inflation expectations eased to 4.2%. Fed hike risk remains, but the data did not justify broad USD strength.

Canadian-dollar-side factors

Canada had no top-tier domestic release. Foreign purchases of Canadian securities slowed to C$7.9bn in May, while Canadians bought C$22.27bn abroad. Those flows were secondary. Oil, Canada-yield repricing and positioning dominated ahead of Monday CPI.

Escalating Gulf conflict drove both oil and haven demand, but the CAD’s oil beta won. Gold rose, Treasuries rallied at the long end, and equities fell. 5. Bank of Canada Analysis

Verified facts On July 15 the Bank held at 2.25%. It projects 2.5% annualized Q2 growth, 0.7% growth in 2026 and 1.8% in 2027–28; inflation is projected to ease toward 2% in early 2027. BoC decision; MPR.

Guidance and pricing: Governing Council judged 2.25% appropriate and removed April’s explicit two-way guidance. The last public market reference implied roughly 15 bps of tightening by December; a reliable next-meeting OIS probability was unavailable, so no precise percentage is invented. September 2 remains a hold base case. The six-to-twelve-month range is hold to one hike; cuts require a material trade, housing or household-demand shock.

  • Inflation: May CPI 3.2% y/y, ex-gasoline 2.2%; trim 2.0%, median 2.1%, common 2.7%. June CPI on July 20 is decisive because oil has surged.
  • Jobs/wages: June employment +18,000, unemployment 6.5%, participation 65.0%, wages +3.3% y/y; gains were almost entirely part-time.
  • Growth/consumer: Q1 GDP flat; April +0.5%, May flash +0.1%; Q1 consumption +0.4%.
  • Housing/debt: June starts 239,000 annualized; mortgage resets and high household leverage restrain discretionary demand.
  • Investment/productivity: capital investment fell 0.7% in Q1; weak capital deepening limits productivity.
  • Fiscal/trade: public and defence spending support demand, but U.S. trade-policy and USMCA review uncertainty restrain investment.

Today’s oil rally raised Canadian yields and supported CAD without changing BoC guidance. A high headline CPI with contained core would be mixed; persistent core inflation would lift hike pricing and CAD more durably.
