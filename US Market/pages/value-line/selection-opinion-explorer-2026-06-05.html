1The Value Line View

Economic & stock-market commentary · p.1661
TL;DR New Fed chair Warsh inherits reaccelerating inflation and an oil shock from the Iran war. The Fed holds June 17, but April minutes show a majority now open to hiking before year-end. The bond market is already doing the tightening — the 30-yr touched its highest since 2007. With indices at records and valuations rich, VL counsels a diversified, high-quality book.
1

2

3
Hawkish tiltFOMC holds at the June 17 meeting, but minutes flag openness to a rate hike later in 2026.
4
Bonds do the workTreasury yields near multi-year highs; 30-yr at its highest since 2007 — the pre-GFC period.
5
Earnings strong (for now)S&P 500 Q1 profit growth ~28%; durability hinges on whether oil eases via an Iran settlement.
6
AI offsetProductivity gains from AI are expected to help cap inflation and costs over time.
Macro read For an index-only book the actionable signal is regime, not names: a hawkish-hold Fed with a live hike risk, a 30-yr at 5.1% (cycle high), and a bond yield now only ~0.3% below the average earnings yield. That compression caps the equity-risk-premium cushion under record-high multiples (DJ Industrials 22.6× est. earnings). VL's own allocation model still leans risk-on at 65% stocks — the tension between a rich tape and a tightening-biased Fed is the thing to size around.

3Model Portfolios

Five editor-run model books · holdings + statistics · pp.1662–1666
Skill read Genuine stock-selection alpha (vs the S&P 500, +10.3% YTD) shows up only in Book I and Book V. III looks good but rides legacy long-holds — its current-year alpha is ~zero; IV is structurally negative. Across the five books the median since-purchase alpha is just +1.4pp: half the holdings haven't truly beaten the index, and the excess is carried by a handful of AI/semis winners.

Macro read For an index lens, the editors' excess return is essentially a single AI-capex exposure, not diversified breadth. If that theme cools, the alpha in Books I/II converges fast; Book IV's lag, by contrast, is a style outcome (low-β, high-yield) rather than a stock-picking error. The leaders (DELL MRVL LRCX NVDA) and laggards (ISRG TMO MDT) are the same names driving sector breadth elsewhere on this page.

How to use this page

Value Line Selection & Opinion Explorer · June 5, 2026 issue

This is an interactive read of one weekly Value Line Selection & Opinion issue. Everything is transcribed from the printed pages and organized into eight scrollable sections (jump via the sticky bar up top).

Sections

  • The View — VL's macro/market commentary distilled to a TL;DR + six numbered points, plus an index-only macro read.
  • Indices — closing averages with 1-week and 12-month moves; click any column header to sort.
  • Model Portfolios — the five editor books (I–V). Switch books with the tabs; the stat tiles update to that book. Filter by text or recommendation; sort any column.
  • Screens — VL's three published stock screens (High-ROE, Income Total-Return, Growth-Low-Risk). Each screen has its own columns.
  • Rates & Fed — rate tiles, an interactive yield-curve chart (3 dates), the asset-allocation donut, plus selected-yields and Fed-data tables.
  • Market Monitor — valuation/yield gauges with a bar showing where today sits inside the 50-week range, against cycle top/bottom anchors.
  • Industry Rotation — best/worst 7 industries over six weeks + financial-strength rating changes.
  • Insiders — notable open-market purchases and sales.

Interaction tips

  • Click a column header to sort; click again to reverse.
  • Use the search box in Portfolios / Screens to filter rows live.
  • Hover the yield-curve dots for exact tenor yields.
  • Press Esc to close this dialog.

Reading orientation

This page is built for a macro, index-only lens: the single-stock tables are breadth and rotation evidence (what's leading, how rich, who's buying), not action lists. The signal worth sizing around is the regime — a hawkish-hold Fed with live hike risk, a cycle-high long end, and record multiples.
