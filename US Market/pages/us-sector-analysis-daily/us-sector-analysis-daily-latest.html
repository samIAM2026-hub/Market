1. Executive Summary

Bottom line: the tape is constructive but not uniformly risk-on. Softer producer inflation and lower Treasury yields supported the index, while bank earnings and a sharp communication-services rebound broadened leadership away from the year-to-date technology and energy winners.

  • The S&P 500 closed 0.4% higher at 7,572.40, within 0.5% of its record, while Nasdaq gained 0.6% and Russell 2000 gained 0.4%.
  • Communication Services led the 11 sectors at +1.73%; Technology lagged at −1.11%, showing a notable one-day handoff inside growth.
  • Financials are the cleanest near-term leader: +0.68% daily, +2.89% weekly and +6.04% monthly, all ahead of SPY.
  • Long-horizon leadership remains concentrated: Energy (+26.37% YTD) and Technology (+26.12%) are far ahead of SPY (+10.69%), but both lost ground on the day.
  • Breadth is mixed, not broken: the latest verified sector technical snapshot had 7 of 11 ETFs above their 50-day averages and 9 above their 200-day averages, but only 45% above 20-day averages.
  • Inflation relief mattered: June final-demand PPI fell 0.3% month over month; the 10-year yield eased to about 4.55% and DXY finished near 100.51.
  • Oil remains the main macro complication: WTI settled near $79.80 and Brent at $84.95 amid the U.S.–Iran conflict, sustaining an inflationary tail risk even as June inflation data cooled.
  • Dominant rotation theme: tactical rotation from semiconductors/long-duration technology toward financials and communication services, with consumer discretionary attempting a recovery while defensives failed to lead.

2. Market Regime

Classification: Mixed, with a risk-on tilt and an inflationary geopolitical overlay. Equity indexes advanced, volatility was normal in the latest verified reading, small caps participated, and yields/dollar eased after PPI. Against that, only five sectors rose, technology sold off, one-year leadership breadth remained narrow, and oil near $80 kept the inflation shock alive.

Evidence: SPY +0.40%, Nasdaq +0.6%, Russell 2000 +0.4%, normal-zone VIX of 16.50 on July 14, and a 4.55% 10-year yield argue against risk-off. Yet sector participation is uneven: only two sectors were outperforming SPY over the trailing year in the latest relative-strength snapshot, and just 45% of sector ETFs were above their 20-day averages. This is a selective, earnings-sensitive advance rather than a fully synchronized cyclical surge.
