1. Executive Summary

Bottom line: Friday converted Thursday’s orderly broadening into a clearer risk-off session. SPY lost 0.99%, ten of eleven sector ETFs fell, volatility rose, and Energy was the only sector to finish higher as oil jumped on renewed Iran-related supply risk.

  • Energy became the unambiguous leader: XLE rose 1.16% Friday, 4.72% for the week and 5.51% over one month; WTI was about $82.30 late Friday. Price history
  • Technology’s correction deepened: XLK fell 1.09%, ended the week down 5.48%, and traded through prior support to a $171.34 low on volume 40.8% above Thursday.
  • Communication Services joined the breakdown: XLC lost 1.78% as Meta, Alphabet and Netflix were reported sharply lower; it remains below prior 50- and 200-day references. Reuters
  • Defensives protected only relatively: XLRE (−0.09%) and XLV (−0.44%) beat SPY, but XLP reversed from a $87.43 intraday high to close down 0.72% on heavier volume.
  • Volatility repriced: Cboe showed VIX at 18.12 at 4:42 p.m. ET, up 8.31% from 16.73; this was elevated, not a panic-level inversion signal. Cboe
  • Rates offered limited relief: official Treasury par yields eased to 4.18% for two years and 4.55% for ten years, but still constrain long-duration equity valuations. U.S. Treasury
  • Macro data were mixed: housing starts rebounded 19.0%, permits fell 3.0%, industrial production rose only 0.1%, and preliminary Michigan sentiment improved to 54.4.
  • Dominant rotation theme: capital moved out of AI/semiconductor and communication exposure into oil-linked cash flows and, secondarily, lower-beta defensives; confirmation is strong in price and relative strength but only partial in defensive volume.

2. Market Regime

Classification: risk-off with an inflationary/geopolitical overlay. The S&P 500 fell 1.01%, Nasdaq 1.40%, Russell 2000 0.41%, and ten of eleven sector ETFs declined. VIX rose above 18 while Brent and WTI jumped. Falling Treasury yields prevented a pure inflation shock, but oil leadership and technology de-rating dominated.

The session differed from July 16: then, nine sector ETFs advanced beneath a weak cap-weighted index; Friday, participation deteriorated to one advancing sector. The official yield curve moved only two basis points lower at both two and ten years, so the growth selloff was driven more by AI valuation/earnings concerns and weekend geopolitical risk than by a new rate spike. 5. Sector Rotation Map

Moving in

Energy: the only positive sector Friday, with positive daily, weekly, monthly and YTD momentum and a 14.2% volume increase from Thursday.

Relative defensives: XLRE and XLV fell less than SPY; XLP/XLF preserved positive weekly returns.

Moving out

Technology and Communication: XLK broke prior support on heavier volume; XLC fell 1.78% with reported pressure in Meta, Alphabet and Netflix.

Consumer beta: XLY ended below both major trend references as risk appetite and oil-related inflation concerns worsened.

Stage and confirmation

Early-to-middle stage, but more forceful: price and relative strength confirm the move; volume confirms selling in XLK/XLC and buying in XLE. Breadth weakened to 1/11 sectors up. Defensive confirmation is mixed because XLV/XLRE volume declined and XLP failed an intraday breakout.
