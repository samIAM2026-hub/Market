Key regime signal to always remember

An S&P 500 rally is healthy when breadth, small caps, and credit confirm it. It is fragile when the index rises mainly because its mega-cap growth sleeve is doing the work. The SPXEW/SPX relationship is your first check every session.

Warning indicator checklist

When several of these trigger simultaneously, do not dismiss them because SPX has not broken yet.

Breadth
SPX up, Equal Weight down
Narrow rally. Acceptable short-term, but dangerous if credit spreads widen or earnings revisions weaken.
Liquidity
Nasdaq up, Russell down
Mega-cap liquidity rally, not broad growth. Risk-on missing its small-cap leg.
Credit
Regional banks down, credit spreads wider
Credit stress. Treat the equity tape with suspicion until banks confirm.
Rates
MOVE rising while VIX stays low
Bond-market stress not yet priced by equities. Often precedes equity volatility by days to weeks.
Cycle
Defensives leading while yields fall
Recession fear, not necessarily bullish easing. Falling yields here reflect growth deterioration.
Macro shock
Oil up + dollar up + yields up + equities down
Inflationary tightening shock — the most painful macro setup. Real incomes squeezed, margins compressed, Fed has no room to ease.
Sentiment
High beta lagging during an index rally
Risk appetite is weaker than headline indices imply. The rally is hiding in quality mega-caps, not broad speculation.
The five indices to watch every day

If you only had time to watch five U.S. indices as a macro trader, these are the ones — and why each earns its place on the minimal viable screen.

1

S&P 500 — SPX

The benchmark price of U.S. risk. The cleanest first-pass read on liquidity and earnings expectations, and the base case against which every other index should be judged. If SPX is moving, you want to know whether that move is being confirmed or contradicted by the other four.

2

Nasdaq 100 — NDX

Tells you whether long-duration growth, AI, and secular earnings leadership are carrying the market. Almost 60% Technology and 0% Financials — the best daily read on how the market is discounting real yields, innovation, and the growth-duration trade.

3

Russell 2000 — RUT

The best listed daily proxy for domestic growth, financing conditions, and credit sensitivity. Small caps feel changes in front-end rates, bank lending, and spread conditions earlier and more brutally than mega-caps. If you want to know whether the market believes in a soft landing rather than just a mega-cap rally, watch Russell 2000.

4

S&P 500 Equal Weight — SPXEW

The best daily breadth and concentration gauge inside the most important benchmark. It tells you whether the tape is broadening or whether the cap-weighted S&P 500 is still resting on a handful of giant names. If SPX is up but Equal Weight is not, the message is caution.

5

MOVE Index

The least conventional choice, but it earns its place because macro breaks often start in rates. MOVE tells you whether the Treasury market is becoming unstable, whether inflation/Fed uncertainty is rising, and whether bond volatility is about to spill into equities, small caps, REITs, and banks. If you could add a sixth line, it would be VIX.

SPX tells you the price of risk. NDX tells you the price of duration-growth. RUT tells you the price of domestic cyclicality and credit. SPX Equal Weight tells you the breadth of participation. MOVE tells you the price of rates instability. That is the minimum viable U.S. macro-equity screen.

Cross-asset overlay signals

Equity indices do not exist in isolation. The six cross-asset inputs below are the truth serum for equity rallies — each confirms or contradicts what the equity tape is telling you.

$Bonds (Treasury yields)

  • Equities ↑ + yields ↑→growth optimism
  • Equities ↓ + yields ↓→recession fear
  • Nasdaq ↓ + real yields ↑→duration compression — worst combo for growth
Pair SPX and Nasdaq against 10Y nominal & TIPS real yields.

FXDollar (DXY)

  • DXY ↑ + Nasdaq & small caps ↓→global liquidity tightening, not rotation
  • DXY ↑ + SPXEW & RUT weak→broad risk-off (sectoral story doesn't fit)
  • Persistent DXY ↑→pressures multinationals, commodities, EM

CRCredit spreads (HY / IG OAS)

  • HY tight/tightening + SPX ↑→durable rally, credit confirming
  • HY widening + SPX ↑→distrust the move — credit sees risk equity hasn't priced
  • Credit confirms, equities lag→buy signal
Credit spreads are the truth serum for equity rallies.

OILOil (WTI)

  • Demand-driven ↑→supports Energy, Industrials, cyclicals
  • Supply/geopolitical ↑→hurts consumer, lifts CPI, caps Fed easing — bearish NDX/REITs
Always ask why oil is moving before drawing a conclusion.

AUGold

  • Gold ↑ + real yields ↓ + DXY ↓→benign easing narrative, constructive for equities
  • Gold ↑ + yields ↑ + DXY ↑ + equities ↓→crisis hedge — macro stress / policy credibility risk
The combination matters more than gold's level alone.

LIQLiquidity breadth check

Watch four proxies together: high beta, Russell 2000, regional banks, Equal Weight.

  • All four confirm→liquidity-driven rally, durable
  • Mega-cap up, four lag/diverge→fragile, narrow rally — treat with caution

Warning stack — read together, not in isolation

Highest-value warning combination

  • Setup: quiet SPX + rising MOVE + wider credit spreads
  • Read: equities haven't priced the rates shock yet
  • Stack confirmation: MOVE, VIX, VVIX, regional banks, HY OAS, NFCI moving together
Don't dismiss the warning just because SPX hasn't broken.

Inflationary tightening shock

  • Setup: oil ↑ + dollar ↑ + yields ↑ + equities ↓ (all at once)
  • Damage: real incomes squeezed, margins compressed, Fed boxed in
  • Tape signature: NDX & REITs lead the decline; Energy is the only sector holding up
