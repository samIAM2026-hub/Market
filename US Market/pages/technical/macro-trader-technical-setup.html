1The purpose of the setup

A macro dashboard exists to answer a tight set of questions in order — not to predict every tick.

The four questions, in order

What the core instruments tell you

Each symbol is a sensor for one part of the machine:

S&P 500 / Nasdaq-100Equity beta (~80% of US market cap)
ES, NQ, YM, RTY24-hr price discovery (real volume)
VIXExpected volatility / fear
DXYDollar liquidity
HYG / LQDCredit risk appetite
Why this matters Any single chart can lie. A market can rise on just five mega-cap names while the other 495 quietly fall apart. The whole point of a cross-asset dashboard is to cross-examine price: is this move confirmed by liquidity, credit and breadth — or is it being rejected by them? When confirmation and price disagree, the disagreement is the signal.

2The timeframe hierarchy

Five lenses on the same market, each with one job. Authority flows downward — the higher timeframe sets the rules; the lower ones only choose the moment. Click a lens to study its role.

▼ More authority at top
Tip: read top→bottom. If two lenses disagree, the higher one wins.
Mental model Think of it like a GPS. The monthly is the country you're in; the weekly is the city; the daily is the neighborhood; the 4H and 1H are the actual turn-by-turn directions. You'd never decide which country to drive to based on a single turn signal — yet that's exactly what traders do when they let a 5-minute candle flip their conviction.

⚠️ The volume nuance most beginners miss

For VWAP, anchored VWAP, and volume profile, use the traded instruments — ES/NQ futures or SPY/QQQ ETFs — not the SPX/NDX spot indexes. Indexes are calculated numbers with no real volume, so any volume-based tool computed on them is meaningless. The futures and ETFs solve this because they actually trade.

3The indicator stack

Four layers, each with a distinct job. The cardinal rule: be consistent — don't switch EMA↔SMA chart-by-chart just because one "looks cleaner."

TrendMoving averages
EMA 20EMA 50MA 100MA 200

EMAs react faster than SMAs. Use 20 & 50 for tactical timing and pullback structure; 100 & 200 to define the medium- and long-term state. Stacked and rising = strong bull.

MomentumSpeed & exhaustion
RSI 14MACDROCStochRSI

RSI = first-pass regime filter. MACD = trend continuation. ROC = acceleration around zero. StochRSI = fast trigger for exhaustion (noisy — never lead with it alone).

VolatilityRisk & sizing

ATR is the most practical tool here: it turns volatility into a concrete stop and position size. VVIX distinguishes orderly from disorderly risk rises.

This layer answers "is the move real?" A rising index with a stalling AD line and lagging RSP is the classic signature of a narrow, fragile rally.

4Breadth, rotation & cross-asset

Breadth tells you whether an index move is real, narrow, or fragile. Cross-asset is where charting becomes actual regime work.

✓ Healthy rally

Index rising · AD line rising · >50% of stocks above intermediate MAs · net new highs positive · equal-weight & small-caps keeping pace.

✕ Narrow rally

SPX/NDX advances but AD line stalls · fewer stocks above 50-day · new highs dry up · RSP & small-caps lag badly. Classic distribution-risk signature.

Cross-asset: the four sensors

RRates

2Y, 10Y, the 2s10s curve. Is the market repricing policy & term premium?

$Dollar (DXY)

Is dollar liquidity tightening or easing? A stronger dollar tends to tighten financial conditions.

CCommodities

WTI, gold, copper. Inflation scare vs growth scare vs geopolitics.

HCredit

HYG vs LQD. Is credit absorbing the shock or amplifying it?

The macro read-map

A synthesis of how the sensors line up into recognizable regimes.

Cross-asset patternMost consistent read
10Y up, curve steepening, DXY firm, oil up, semis pressured, VIX risingInflation scare / duration unwind
Yields down hard, copper & oil down, gold firm, defensives lead, credit weakeningGrowth scare
DXY softer, VIX falling, HYG firm, cyclicals & semis lead, breadth improvingLiquidity rally / risk-on
VIX & VVIX up, DXY up, HYG underperforming LQD, breadth weak, defensives outperformHard risk-off
Sector rotation in one breath Map relative strength vs SPY onto the business cycle. Early/recovery: discretionary, financials, real estate, industrials, materials. Mid-cycle: tech & semis lead as capex recovers. Late-cycle: energy and selective defensives as inflation/rates dominate. Recession/scare: staples, utilities, health care — demand that doesn't flinch. Watch XLK/SMH for growth leadership, XLF/XLI for cyclical confirmation, XLP/XLU/XLV as the defensive tell.

6The 8 regimes & decision rules

The full model: chart shape + what confirms it + what a macro trader actually does. Not a claim markets fit neat boxes — a tool to force consistent behavior.

The four risk rules Add risk only when daily & weekly point the same way and breadth isn't contradicting price. Reduce when the index loses 20 & 50 together on rising volatility, or the rally narrows to a few mega-caps. Hedge when the higher-timeframe bull is intact but rates/oil/event risk threaten a vol shock. Hold cash when price is below the 200-day, breadth is poor, credit isn't confirming, and every bounce is just mean-reversion inside a broken structure.

Marking levels systematically (not artistically)

Support/resistance are supply–demand zones, not magic lines. Mark: prior highs/lows · unfilled gaps · 20/50/200-day MAs · anchored VWAP from major events or swing lows · fixed-range volume profile on big legs · Fibonacci 38.2 / 50 / 61.8% · round numbers.

Highest-probability zones cluster. When a rising 50-day EMA, an anchored VWAP from the last CPI gap, and a 38.2% retracement all land in the same area — that confluence is where you want to act.
