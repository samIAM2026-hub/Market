Investment Framework

DCA Investment Framework & Daily Review System

A complete, repeatable system to monitor U.S. equity markets, classify market regime, and adjust dollar-cost averaging — built for the long-term investor who wants to separate signal from noise.

Section 01 · Foundation

Investment Objective

Why this framework exists and how it should be used by a long-term DCA investor who also wants real understanding of macro conditions, market risk, and sector rotation.

Purpose

The framework is a decision system, not a forecasting tool. Its purpose is to convert daily noise — futures moves, headlines, individual data points — into a small set of actionable signals that tell the investor (a) what regime the market is in, (b) whether risk is rising or falling, and (c) what to do with the next DCA tranche. It is built to be used every trading day, in under 15 minutes once routine, and to keep behavior consistent across emotional market environments.

For the long-term DCA investor

The base case is to keep buying. Time in market beats market timing over multi-decade horizons. This framework does not exist to make tactical "in/out" calls — it exists to size the next contribution, decide whether to split it, and protect against the two failure modes that destroy DCA outcomes: panic selling in drawdowns and over-deploying cash at the top of a melt-up.

For the macro-aware investor

Macro doesn't tell you what stock to buy. It tells you which environment you're in — and therefore what kind of mistakes are most expensive. In a liquidity-driven rally, the mistake is sitting in cash. In a late-cycle melt-up, the mistake is leveraging up. In a recessionary risk-off, the mistake is panic selling at the bottom. The framework's job is to keep these mistakes out of the playbook.

Six things the framework should produce

  1. A structured daily collection of market data.
  2. A read on the current market regime.
  3. A short list of what matters today.
  4. A filter that separates noise from signal.
  5. A read on whether risk is rising or falling.
  6. A concrete DCA action for the day or week.

Default action

When nothing significant has changed, the correct answer is almost always "continue normal DCA." Deviation from base case requires evidence, not vibes. The framework's bias is toward inaction unless multiple indicators agree.

What this framework will not do

It will not predict tops or bottoms. It will not tell you which individual stocks to buy. It will not eliminate drawdowns. It is a process, not a crystal ball — and a good process consistently applied beats an excellent forecast occasionally applied.

Section 02 · Classification

Market Regime Framework

Every trading day, classify the market into exactly one of nine regimes. The classification drives everything downstream — the data you weight, the headlines you ignore, and the DCA action you take. Click any regime to expand its signals, behavior pattern, risks, and DCA response.

How to choose between regimes

Most days, two regimes feel plausible. Pick the one that explains the most evidence (price, breadth, credit, vol, rates). If you can't decide between two adjacent regimes, default to the more cautious one — the cost of overestimating risk in a DCA framework is small (you keep some cash), the cost of underestimating it is large (you over-deploy near a top).

Section 03 · Inputs

Key Data to Monitor

Seven categories of inputs. Not every indicator updates daily — but every one of these should be on your watch list, with a known source and a known threshold for "this matters." The "Why" column tells you the question each indicator answers; the "Signal" column tells you what to interpret.

Discipline rule

Watch the indicator, not the headline about the indicator. A bond auction with weak demand matters more than a pundit warning that "bonds might sell off." Levels and trends in the data are the truth — narratives change daily.

Section 07 · Decision Engine

DCA Decision Rules

Eight possible actions. Each card lists the market conditions that support the action, the confirming indicators, the risks of taking it, and how long to hold the stance before re-evaluating. Discipline: at most one deviation from base case at a time.

Section 08 · Behavior Guardrails

Risk Management Rules

The framework's most important section. Most DCA failures are behavioral, not analytical. These six rules exist to keep emotion from overriding process during the moments when emotion most wants to.

The two unforgivable mistakes

1. Selling at the bottom of a correction because the headlines feel worst there. 2. Deploying all reserves at the top of a melt-up because FOMO peaks there. The entire framework is designed to keep you from either one — every rule above is in service of that.

Section 09 · Synthesis

Important: the score is a discipline tool, not a trading signal

Don't change DCA based on a single day's score. Require two consecutive days in the same bucket before adjusting. The score's job is to flag inconsistency between gut and evidence — when your instinct says "buy aggressively" but the score is −5, the score wins.

How to use this framework

Daily flow (15 minutes once routine)

  • Morning (5 min): Run the AM Checklist (Tab 4). Note overnight tone, futures, key macro events. Form a one-sentence risk view.
  • Midday (optional): Quick glance at AM view — is it still holding?
  • Evening (8 min): Run the PM Checklist (Tab 5). Fill the Daily Output (Tab 9). Update the Scoring widget (Tab 10).
  • Done. If the score crosses a threshold for two consecutive days, adjust DCA per the rules in Tab 7.

Weekly flow (Saturday, 20 min)

  • Open the Weekly Review (Tab 6). Synthesize the five daily outputs into pillar-level trend calls.
  • Re-read the Regime Framework (Tab 2). Is the regime call still correct? Has anything shifted?
  • Save the snapshot. Schedule the week's DCA tranches based on the action chosen in the Decision Engine (Tab 7).

Tab guide

  • Tab 1 — Objective: Read once. Re-read in moments of doubt.
  • Tab 2 — Regimes: Reference daily. Pick exactly one.
  • Tab 3 — Data: Your indicator universe. Build muscle memory for the "why" and "signal" columns.
  • Tabs 4 & 5 — Checklists: Daily checkable lists. Progress saves in your browser.
  • Tab 6 — Weekly: Fillable template. Save snapshots and compare week-over-week.
  • Tab 7 — DCA Rules: Eight actions. At most one deviation from base case at a time.
  • Tab 8 — Risk: The behavior guardrails. Re-read before any non-routine action.
  • Tab 9 — Daily Output: End-of-day synthesis. Copy as text and archive in a notes app.
  • Tab 10 — Scoring: The discipline check. Two consecutive days in a bucket = action.

Three principles

  • Process over prediction. The score is a thermometer, not a forecast.
  • Require multi-signal confirmation. One indicator never changes DCA. Two or three across categories might.
  • Default to base case. When in doubt, continue normal DCA. Deviation costs are usually higher than complacency costs for a long-horizon investor.

Data: snapshots persist in your browser only

Checklist progress, weekly snapshots, daily outputs, and score history are saved in your browser's local storage. They do not leave your computer. To archive long-term, use the Copy as text button and paste into a notes app or spreadsheet.

Important caveat

This framework is a personal decision-support tool. It is not investment advice. Markets routinely defy any framework — the value here is consistency of process, not accuracy of prediction. Combine with broad diversification, a long horizon, and tolerance for drawdowns.
