Common mistakes & false signals
Six errors that show up in this kind of environment
MistakeThe correction
Treating VIX alone as a stress gaugeLow-to-mid teens can mean complacency; high teens can still be normal. Pair with credit and breadth.
Using yield-curve steepening as automatically bullishInflation/term-premium steepening ≠ easier-policy steepening into a soft landing.
Letting valuation become a timing triggerExpensive markets can stay expensive for long periods, especially when earnings are accelerating.
Ignoring equal-weight and small-cap confirmationCap-weighted SPX can look far healthier than the average stock. Put RSP / IWM on screen 1.
Confusing a growth scare with an inflation shockGrowth scare → yields fall. Inflation shock → yields, oil, USD rise together. May 21–22's oil reversal reduced inflation-shock pressure, but the risk has not disappeared.
Using sentiment without creditPanic + tight spreads = opportunity. Panic + widening spreads = something worse may be starting.
Reading nominal data as real strengthUnder sticky CPI, nominal retail sales and corporate revenue can keep rising while volumes shrink. Always check real GDP and unit growth before declaring earnings durable — otherwise stagflation hides inside strong-looking nominals.
Treating GEX levels as hard railsCall Wall / Put Wall / Zero Gamma are a real-time pressure map of dealer inventory, not predictive price targets. 0DTE flows can shift the intraday Zero Gamma fast — verify daily, never read in isolation from credit and trend.
Acknowledged limits
What this framework can't see well without paid data

Dealer gamma, CTA positioning, CDX basis, Treasury order-book depth, FRA-OIS, single-name CDS. The framework emphasises the highest-confidence public indicators and uses paid-style signals only when public proxies still make decisions useful.

User manual — how to use this page

Five interactive widgets, nine tabs. Open this manual any time with the floating "?" button.

What this page is for

Purpose · Turn a 9-page macro framework into a daily tool. Score what you see, classify any selloff, and pick a DCA stance — without re-reading the source report each time.

Three things to do first

  • Score the day. Click any pillar in the sticky top strip to cycle +1 → 0 → −1. The total score on the right updates instantly and changes the regime label.
  • If the tape is ugly, classify it. Use the selloff classifier on the Overview tab — pick how yields/credit/USD/oil/banks are behaving and it returns a regime label.
  • Pick a DCA mode. On the DCA Matrix tab, click each of the five mode cards to read its conditions before sizing a contribution.

What's saved between sessions

Nothing. Pillar scores reset every reload — by design. Forcing you to re-score each day is part of the discipline.

Tip · Hover any "?" pill on the page for a quick hint without opening this manual.

6-pillar scorecard (sticky top strip)

Purpose · Compress today's market into one number from −6 to +6. The total drives the regime label and DCA action shown on the right of the strip.

What each pillar means and how to score it

PillarWhat to look atHow to score
TrendSPX price vs 20-, 50-, 200-day MAs; weekly trend+1 above all key MAs · 0 mixed · −1 below 200d with failed rallies
BreadthAdvance/decline line, % above 50-day, RSP vs SPY, IWM vs SPY+1 A/D confirming & equal-weight rising · 0 mixed · −1 new lows expanding or RSP/IWM diverging
Rates10Y real yield, 2Y, curve shape, MOVE index+1 falling/stable real yields · 0 drifting · −1 rising real yields with rising oil/USD
CreditHY OAS, IG OAS, bank/KRE stocks vs SPX+1 spreads tight · 0 mild widening · −1 fast widening or bank stress
LiquidityNFCI/ANFCI, RRP, TGA, reserves, EFFR/SOFR, DXY+1 conditions loose · 0 neutral · −1 tightening with reserve drains
EarningsForward EPS revisions, beat rate, guidance ratio+1 revisions up · 0 flat · −1 rolling over

How the total maps to action

  • +4 to +6 — risk-on; favour growth/cyclicals; normal DCA
  • +1 to +3 — constructive but selective; normal DCA or weekly tranches
  • −1 to 0 — choppy / late-cycle; keep DCA on, slow the pace
  • −2 to −4 — correction risk rising; wait for better prices or confirmation
  • −5 to −6 — recession/stress regime; preserve cash; buy only on capitulation with stabilising credit
Tip · If you can't decide between two scores, default to the lower one. The framework is designed to slow you down at extension highs.

Selloff classifier (Overview tab)

Purpose · On a down day, force yourself to read the drivers before reacting. The classifier maps five inputs to one of six selloff types — the response is different for each.

What each entry means

EntryWhat to checkDecision relevance
1 · YieldsDirection of 10Y and 2Y today. Up if both rising more than ~3-5 bps.Yields up + everything else weak → likely inflation/rates. Yields down → growth scare.
2 · Credit (HY OAS)Is the high-yield spread tight, modestly wider, or blowing out?Tight = positioning shakeout. Blowing = credit event — the most decision-critical entry.
3 · US DollarDXY direction today. Up if bid hard; soft if losing ground.USD up + yields up + oil up → inflation shock. USD up hard alone → possible liquidity stress.
4 · Oil / commoditiesWTI / Brent / copper. Up if sharply higher on geopolitics or supply.Oil up confirms inflation shock. Oil down argues growth scare or disinflation pullback.
5 · Banks (KRE)Are regional/large banks underperforming SPX hard?Banks cracking with falling yields → recession/credit event. Banks cracking with rising long yields → duration/term-premium shock.

What the verdict tells you

  • Normal pullback / technical correction — credit refuses to widen; DCA opportunity if breadth gets oversold
  • Growth scare — yields fall; defensives lead; watch PMIs & claims
  • Inflation shock — yields, oil, USD all up; cut beta in QQQ/semis; tranche DCA
  • Credit event — spreads jump and banks lead lower; raise hedges fast
  • Liquidity crisis — USD up hard with funding stress; assume systematic deleveraging
Tip · If two answers seem true, pick the more pessimistic one. The classifier is meant to slow you down, not give comfort.

DCA mode picker (DCA matrix tab)

Purpose · Pick a contribution size that matches the regime — designed to stop you buying most aggressively into euphoric extensions and stopping just when expected returns improve.

What each mode is for

ModeWhen to pick itWorth remembering
Normal DCAAbove 200d; breadth healthy; spreads tight; revisions stableYour default state. Don't try to time every wiggle.
Increase5–10% pullback in an intact uptrend; VIX elevated; spreads stableThe sweet spot for buying without a recession overlay.
Aggressively increase10–20% correction; sentiment capitulation; no credit/funding accidentWhere long-run returns improve most. Requires conviction.
Tranche & waitExtended above trend; narrow leadership; yields & USD & oil rising (today)You don't stop compounding — you stop rewarding euphoria.
Hold extra cashBelow 200d AND spreads widening AND revisions falling AND recession risk risingMultiple pillars must line up. Cash is dangerous on false breakdowns.
Tip · You should land in Tranche & wait or Normal DCA most of the year. If you're picking the extremes weekly, you're probably overreacting.
