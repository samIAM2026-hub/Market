Executive summary

Strong insurance and bank fundamentals met a harsher growth-stock tape

  • Travelers led: core EPS beat consensus by 85.6%, combined ratio reached 83.6%, and shares rose 8.25%.
  • Intuitive was the sharpest negative: revenue/EPS beat, procedures grew 16%, yet unchanged procedure guidance and coverage concerns drove a 14.19% fall.
  • Regional banks were constructive but selective: Truist and Regions beat and rose; Fifth Third’s core strength was offset by acquisition noise and integration risk.
  • Netflix’s first full session confirmed the reset: shares fell 7.26% as a small revenue miss and softer Q3 outlook outweighed the EPS beat.
  • Index implication is Mixed: earnings breadth supports growth, while valuation and forward-guidance tolerance deteriorated.

Its Business Insurance, Bond & Specialty and Personal Insurance segments serve companies, public entities and households, mainly in the U.S. Premium growth, renewal pricing, retention, loss-cost inflation, catastrophe losses, reserve development, investment income and capital return drive earnings. Quality is not High because $578 million of favorable prior-year reserve development and unusually low cats are volatile.

Tier 1 · Health Care / Health Care Equipment

Intuitive Surgical ISRG · Q2 2026

Result 8/10 · Quality Medium-high · Guidance Neutral · Thesis unchanged · Risk-reward Unattractive · Confidence High

Company profile

Intuitive develops robotic-assisted surgical platforms, principally da Vinci systems for soft-tissue surgery and Ion for minimally invasive lung biopsy. It sells or leases systems to hospitals and earns recurring revenue from instruments, accessories, service and usage globally.
