📅 May 18–22 Analysis
Executive Summary
✅

Better than expected across the board. ~94% of S&P 500 companies have reported, with 84% beating EPS and 81% beating revenue. Blended EPS growth of +28.4% — the strongest broad earnings pulse since late 2021.

📈

Earnings are supporting the rally. By May 29, the S&P 500 closed at 7,580.06, while the Nasdaq 100 and Russell 2000 remained strongly positive YTD. The tape is still growth-led, but participation outside mega-cap tech has improved.

🤖

AI thesis confirmed by revenue, not just narrative. Alphabet, Microsoft, Amazon, and NVIDIA all posted powerful cloud/AI-driven growth. NVIDIA's Q1 FY27 data-center revenue reached $75.2B, confirming the AI infrastructure cycle remains intact.

💰

Corporate profitability improving. S&P 500 net profit margin 14.8% — highest in the FactSet tracking history. YoY improvement is roughly +200bps vs Q1 2025. Main pressure point remains cash intensity from AI capital spending.

⚠️

Valuation is the main constraint. Forward P/E 21.1×, above both 5-year and 10-year averages. With the 10-year now at 4.45%, the forward earnings-yield cushion is only ~30bp — still an unusually slim margin of safety.

Weekly Macro Synthesis

The week's prints add up to a soft-landing-confirming macro tape: AI capex still scaling, consumer intact, software demand durable, cyclicals bottoming but not inflecting, rates still capping the multiple.

AI capex (NVDA + ADI) — NVDA's $81.6B revenue, $75.2B data-center, and ~$91B Q2 guide confirm hyperscaler AI capex remains in expansion mode; ADI broadens the signal into analog/industrial semis. Net: tech sector contribution to S&P EPS stays elevated, keeping QQQ/SMH leadership intact.

Consumer (WMT, TGT, HD, LOW, TJX) — WMT's US comps +4.1%, eCom +26%, ad rev +37% signals durable low-end household spending — no recession in real-time retail data. TGT (+5.6% comps, +4.4% traffic) confirms discretionary stabilization. HD/LOW at +0.6% comps mark a housing floor without inflection. TJX strength confirms K-shape / trade-down dynamics persist. Net: soft-landing base case strengthened, XLP-over-XLY tilt warranted, XHB/IWM lag continues until rates fall.

Software / corporate IT (INTU, WDAY) — INTU raised FY26 guide; WDAY reported Q1 FY27 Non-GAAP EPS of $2.66 (vs. $2.56 est.) and revenue of $2.542B (+13.5% YoY), reiterating its FY27 subscription revenue guide while raising non-GAAP operating margin guidance to 30.5%. WDAY surged ~9.5% in after-hours. Translation: enterprise + SMB IT budgets are intact and margin expansion is accelerating. Bullish for services PMI and IGV/XLK index-level earnings durability.

Industrial / rates (DE, 10Y at 4.45%) — DE reported Q2 FY26 GAAP EPS of $6.55 (vs. $5.81 est.) on revenues of $13.37B (vs. $11.6B est.), but the stock fell 7.5% as Production & Precision Ag segment sales fell 14% YoY, signaling a clear ag-cycle trough. The 10Y at 4.45% (May 29) caps index multiples, meaning cyclical rotation (QQQ → XLI/IWM) remains premature.

Gaming / Consumer Tech (TTWO) — TTWO beat bookings guidance ($1.58B vs. $1.50B est.) and confirmed Grand Theft Auto VI release date for November 19, 2026, while projecting ~20% bookings growth in FY27. The stock rose ~7% after-hours on relief that release window has not slipped. Net: positive read-through for broader gaming and interactive media (EA, SONY).

Index-level read: stay constructive on SPY/QQQ via earnings strength, maintain defensive/growth software tilt (XLP, IGV/XLK), keep TLT/IEF as hedge for any growth scare, underweight IWM and XHB until rates decline.

Key Themes From Earnings Calls

What management teams emphasized this season, distilled to one read per theme with a directional sentiment. Net effect across themes is favorable-to-neutral for markets — AI/cloud demand the standout positive; consumer, housing and labor the cautionary notes.

🤖

POSITIVE AI & Related Capex

Most-cited theme of the season. Semis-to-servers-to-software reported massive AI-driven demand; NVDA's guide implied a large data-center capex leap, CAT cited an "AI data-center boom" for power equipment, Oracle guiding toward ~$90B revenue by 2027.

☁️

POSITIVE Cloud Spending

Robust across providers: Azure +40%, Google Cloud +63%, AWS +28%. No broad complaints of enterprise IT cuts — calls emphasized ongoing digital transformation.

🏗️

POSITIVE Data-Center / Backbone Capex

Spend on servers, networking and industrial gear rising. Broadcom sold $8.4B of AI chips (+106%); data-center REITs raised outlooks. Sustained IT infrastructure investment.

🛒

NEUTRAL → NEG. Consumer Spending

Mixed signals. Several retailers note trade-down and energy-cost sensitivity (WMT flagged ~$175M fuel impact). Homebuilders beat but warned of margin give-back via incentives. Demand intact but pressured by inflation.

NEUTRAL Credit Quality / Loan Growth

Banks say credit remains solid; no widespread loss warnings. Modest loan growth as high rates slow refinancing. Credit still a tailwind for banks without material risk.

📣

POSITIVE Advertising Demand

Online ad markets strong. Meta: impressions +19%, price +12%. Alphabet total revenue +22% on core ad strength. Digital ad demand held up despite budget concerns.

💻

POSITIVE Enterprise Software / IT

Many SaaS names noted accelerated deals; AI/automation boosting software spend (Oracle, IBM optimistic). Few warnings of corporate IT pullbacks.

⚙️

POSITIVE Industrial Demand

CAT and BA emphasized firm backlogs; construction and power segments at historically high orders tied to infrastructure and datacenters. Machinery producers report solid global demand.

🏠

NEGATIVE Housing / Real Estate

High mortgage rates slowing starts. D.R. Horton expects lower margins next quarter on incentives; apartment REITs note rising vacancies. Industrial/logistics REITs healthier, but housing broadly under strain.

🛢️

MIXED Energy Demand

Producers cite high demand and prices, but results mixed (Exxon profit fell despite oil >$90). Utilities note higher fuel costs but have rate-hike pricing power. Supply disruptions add volatility.

🚢

MIXED → NEUT. Tariffs / Trade / Supply Chains

A few names (CAT, DOW) flagged higher tariff and shipping costs; some improvements noted. CEOs broadly feel supply chains easing — few inventory issues — but supplier price inflation persists.

👷

MIXED → NEG. Labor Costs

Wage inflation persists (mid-to-high single digits); tech firms restructuring to manage staffing costs. One of the main cost pressures cited — a modest margin headwind ahead.

🏷️

POSITIVE Pricing Power

Many companies still able to raise prices without losing customers. Staples and pharma benefited; airlines/autos passed fuel and material costs through. Helped offset cost inflation.

💸

POSITIVE Capital Returns / Buybacks

Hefty buybacks announced post-earnings (Apple $100B, Nvidia $25B, etc.), signaling management confidence and boosting EPS.

Net read: The dominance of AI / cloud / data-center demand is the strong positive thread; pricing power, ads, software, industrials and buybacks reinforce it. The cautionary cluster — consumer, housing, labor — is real but contained, consistent with a soft-landing rather than a downturn.

Bullish · Neutral · Bearish Scenarios
🟢 Bullish

Guidance keeps rising (2026 EPS >20%) and beats broaden beyond tech into mid-caps. Inflation eases → Fed cuts sooner; long rates fall or stabilize. AI revenues (cloud, software, chips) ramp as expected; consumer rebounds modestly. Valuations stay justified and the market extends its run.

⚪ Neutral / Base

Most results and guidance already priced in. Strong earnings keep prices elevated but further gains stall. Mixed guidance keeps investors cautious; growth slows to trend. High valuations cap upside; leadership stays narrow (tech/AI). Stocks drift sideways in a high range.

🔴 Bearish

Analysts cut 2026 estimates as forward weakness emerges; guidance softens. Inflation re-accelerates → Fed higher-for-longer, yields spike. Margins compress; "sell the news" on AI hype. A shock (geopolitics, China, credit) hits. P/E multiples contract and SPX gives back gains.
