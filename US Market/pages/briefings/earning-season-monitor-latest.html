Decision brief

Executive summary

  1. Bank results were broadly better than published expectations. Goldman Sachs produced the cleanest upside, with EPS 45% above consensus and revenue 23% above; JPMorgan’s operating EPS beat by about 10% before $1.56 of significant-item EPS.
  2. Trading and investment banking, not reserve releases, drove the bank upside. Goldman’s Global Banking & Markets revenue rose 53% year over year; Bank of America’s sales-and-trading revenue rose 33%; Wells Fargo’s Markets revenue rose 24%.
  3. Credit stayed benign. Wells Fargo’s net charge-off rate fell to 0.34% from 0.44%, Bank of America’s net charge-offs declined, and the first bank cohort described consumers as resilient. This is a constructive macro signal, though a favorable present-tense reading rather than a recession guarantee.
  4. Guidance separated the stocks. JPMorgan raised 2026 net-interest-income guidance to roughly $105.5 billion. Wells Fargo held its full-year outlook despite a strong print, and Citi maintained 10%–11% 2026 RoTCE guidance; both shares fell.
  5. IBM was the decisive disappointment. Preliminary revenue of $17.2 billion missed consensus by about 3.7%, with Software growth only 5% and Infrastructure down 7%. The 25.2% share decline was the company’s worst in decades and created a negative read-through for traditional enterprise software and services.
  6. Fastenal showed healthy industrial demand but weaker margin quality. Sales grew 14.7% and exceeded consensus, yet gross margin fell 75 basis points and cash conversion was only 69%; the stock fell 2.8%.
  7. Market implication: mixed-positive. The S&P 500 rose 0.4% as cooler inflation and strong bank earnings outweighed IBM. The earnings signal supports cyclicals and capital-markets activity, but IBM demonstrates that high expectations and AI-related budget shifts can punish execution misses severely.

Scope and method

The S&P 500 earnings-calendar check identified six scheduled reporters on July 14—JPMorgan Chase, Bank of America, Wells Fargo, Citigroup, Goldman Sachs and Fastenal—and one unscheduled preliminary release from IBM. No S&P 500 constituent was scheduled to report after the July 14 close. Sector and industry labels use current GICS classifications.

Company facts are taken from company releases, SEC-filed materials and company-distributed financial supplements. Consensus comparisons use public FactSet, LSEG, Zacks and Investing.com snapshots. Calculations are labeled as analyst calculations.

Data limitations

What is not yet available

  • IBM’s figures are preliminary; final results and its earnings call are scheduled for July 22.
  • Several bank 10-Qs and complete official call transcripts were not posted by cutoff. Call-tone analysis is therefore limited to releases and reputable same-day summaries.
  • No proprietary real-time consensus, positioning, options, whisper, estimate-revision or normalized volume feed was available.
  • Consensus providers differ on revenue definitions, especially for banks. Reported-versus-consensus comparisons use the cited provider’s basis and should not be mixed across sources.
  • Formal valuation scenarios and price targets are omitted because a consistent post-close price/forward-estimate set was not verifiable for every company.

Industry and market

Industry and sector read-throughs

Financials

The first bank cohort points to a favorable combination of resilient consumers, loan growth, still-benign credit and unusually strong capital-markets activity. Results favor diversified banks and investment banks, but Citi and Wells Fargo show that simple beats are insufficient when guidance is not raised. Trading revenue is cyclical and should not be capitalized as a permanent run rate.

Technology

IBM indicates that AI spending can cannibalize traditional software, mainframe and services budgets over short windows. Delayed large deals and transaction-processing weakness are negative for legacy enterprise vendors and IT services. The broad S&P technology signal remains mixed because semiconductor leaders rebounded sharply the same day.

Industrials

Fastenal’s 14.7% daily-sales growth and larger-customer wins suggest improving manufacturing and maintenance demand. However, unfavorable price/cost and customer mix transferred some of that growth away from gross margin, a warning for distributors with less SG&A leverage.

Overall S&P 500 earnings-growth and market implication: mixed-positive

Public FactSet data cited by Axios put expected Q2 S&P 500 EPS growth at 22.5%, with Energy expected to contribute disproportionally. Today’s bank prints support that high aggregate growth expectation, while IBM shows the market will punish misses severely. The S&P 500’s 0.4% gain to 7,543.59 was also aided by cooler inflation, so the index move should not be attributed to earnings alone.
